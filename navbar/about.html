Engagements pour l'environnement

Des produits qui durent

The Tasman is produced with durable recycled cast iron and designed to withstand the test of time. Although the average lifespan of non-stick cookware is around 2 years, Dutch Ovens are praised for their longevity all over the world. Our Tasman is incredibly strong and will last for generations. A pan with such a long lifespan reduces a lot of impact on nature.

Le recyclage et la valorisation des déchets

We choose only recyclable packaging options. When you receive The Tasman, it will be in a recycled cardboard box, which you will be able to fully recycle or reuse (we suggest re-using as we chose the packaging design to let the box become a nice and functional inhouse storage solution + finding a different use for the box is the epitome of sustainability).

Des matières durables

Our Tasman is made of 95% recycled material. We choose to work with recycled materials to eliminate piles and piles of scrap metal together. That’s why The Tasman is made from old polluting cars, among other things. This is how we can make our Dutch Oven & Grill without mining one single iron ore. We think this is important because mining of iron ore has a negative impact on the environment. As the mining method is usually open cast, it degrades natural landscapes, surfaces and groundwater, Flora and Fauna, as well as the ambient air quality within the mining area and its environs.

Sustainable Distribution

Usually product packaging is not made to actually ship the product in. Therefore, there is often a "shipping box", giving you a box in a box, full of air, which significantly increases the environmental impact. Our biodegradable cardboard shipping box is simultaneously the "packaging" of The Tasman, we do not ship a box in a box. And therefore no 'air' either. By doing so, we ensure that we 're cutting back 60% percent of packaging materials. And the materials we do use can be recycled or reused. We've been using our boxes like this since our very first Kickstarter campaign for the Blackbeard, which earned us a spot as a finalist in the Dutch Packaging awards.
